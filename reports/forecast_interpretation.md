# Forecast Interpretation (Access & Usage, 2025–2027)

## What the model predicts (base, event-augmented)
- **Access (account ownership):** gradual gains from the 49% (2024) base — continued progress but **well below** stretch NFIS-style ambitions without rural breakthroughs.
- **Usage (digital payments):** continued climb from 35% (2024), with more upside than Access because interoperability, merchant QR, and active-use conversion affect payment behavior directly.

Exact point estimates and intervals are in `models/forecast_summary.csv` and Task 4 notebook outputs.

## Scenarios
| Scenario | Narrative |
|----------|-----------|
| Pessimistic | Affordability shocks, weak active-use conversion, rural stall |
| Base | Trend + tempered event effects |
| Optimistic | Strong interoperability + merchant acceptance + narrowing gender/rural gaps |

## Highest-impact events
1. Telebirr / M-Pesa product launches (Usage & MM)
2. EthSwitch interoperability / EthioPay rails
3. Merchant QR acceptance expansion
4. Fayda digital ID (medium-term Access/KYC)

## Key uncertainties
- Findex sparsity and medium-confidence historical Usage anchors
- Pass-through from registered to survey-active users
- Macro/FX effects on device and data affordability
- Policy implementation intensity

Wide confidence bands are intentional and appropriate.
