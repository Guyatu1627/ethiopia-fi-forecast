# Data Quality Assessment

## Strengths
- Core Access series (`ACC_OWNERSHIP`) has five Findex anchors (2011–2024).
- Event catalog covers major product, policy, infrastructure, and milestone shocks.
- Impact links encode direction, magnitude, lag, and evidence basis.
- Enrichment adds Usage history, gender/urban-rural splits, and registered–survey gap.

## Limitations
1. **Sparse Findex cadence** (every ~3 years) limits classical time-series identification.
2. **Historical Usage points (2014/2017/2021)** are medium-confidence compilations, not always a single published cell.
3. **Operator metrics ≠ survey definitions** — registered users overstate inclusion.
4. **Enabler series** (4G, affordability, Fayda) are short and sometimes projected.
5. **Impact magnitudes** are modeled estimates (local + comparable-country), not causal RCTs.
6. **No rich regional panel** beyond urban/rural national splits.

## Implications for modeling
- Prefer transparent trend + event-augmented scenarios with **wide intervals**.
- Down-scale Access event shocks relative to operator-era narratives.
- Treat 2025–2027 forecasts as decision-support ranges, not precise point predictions.
